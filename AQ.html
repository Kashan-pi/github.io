Dr.Abdul Qadeer Khan: 'Father of Pakistan's Nuclear Bomb'

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan is a Pakistani nuclear physicist and a metallurgical engineer, widely known as the father of Pakistan's nuclear bomb.

Born on April 27, 1936, in Bhopal, India, Dr. Khan played a crucial role in Pakistan's nuclear weapons program. He was responsible for transforming Pakistan into a nuclear power in 1998.

Despite controversy surrounding his activities, Dr. Khan remains a prominent figure in Pakistani history for his contributions to the country's defense capabilities.

This website aims to highlight his achievements and contributions to science and technology.

Successful Testing Of First Nuclear Device:

On May 28, 1998, Pakistan successfully tested its first nuclear device and became the first country in the muslim world to have nuclear power. This made Pakistan untouchable! And the credit goes to Dr. Abdul Qadir.

Key Achievements:

  • Development of Pakistan's first nuclear weapons.
  • Establishment of the Khan Research Laboratories (KRL).
  • Recipient of numerous awards for his work in nuclear physics.

Highest Civil Award:

Dr. Abdul Qadir Khan was awarded with the highest civil award Nishan E Pakistan and is the only Pakistani to receive this award twice along with this he was also awarded with Hilal E Imtiaz in 1990.

Death:

The man regarded as the "Father of Pakistan's Nuclear Bomb", Dr Abdul Qadeer Khan, has died aged 85 after being hospitalised with Covid-19 on 10 October 2021.Dr Khan was hailed as a national hero for transforming his country into the world's first Islamic nuclear power.
